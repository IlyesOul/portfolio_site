Machine Learning Independent Study 2023/2024 + 2024/2025

2023/2024 + 2024/2025 study: core ml techniques

across two independent studies, i built a progression from core machine learning concepts to advanced, production-level systems. in the first, i focused on supervised and unsupervised learning through hands-on projects like a personalized to-do list organizer and a stock clustering model, later applying optimization and building neural networks in keras, including an lstm for stock prediction. the second served as a continuation, where i shifted toward designing end-to-end ml pipelines with preprocessing, feature engineering, boosting, and more advanced neural network architectures, emphasizing scalability and real-world applicability.

How & Why I made it

i designed the full curriculum for each study and, after presenting them to my computer science teacher for approval, was able to dedicate several class period to them. i created them as a way to dive deeper into a field I’m passionate about and one that’s highly relevant to solving real-world problems and opportunities.

Curriculum

  1. Unit 1: Supervised Learning
  2. Unit 2: Unsupervised Learning
  3. Unit 3: Model Evaluation, model optimization, and data preprocessing
  4. Unit 4: Neural Networks
  5. Unit 5: Experimental/Application programs
  6. Unit 6: (24/25): Neural Network techniques
  7. Unit 7: ML Pipelines
  8. Unit 8: NLP (in progress)

Program Integration

whenever i learn a new topic through the study, i either create projects that apply it, or i integrate it into an existing program. such is the case with programs like the prioritized to-do list, theta terminal, intellivest, and big ideas in the future.

Work in progress

there are some subjects in the study that I wasn't able to reach in time, and i plan to continue working on them in the future. additionally, whenever i learn a subejct that is similar to the contents of either study, I add it to the curriculum and integrate it into the projects I've already completed.

College Dual Enrollment Courses

during my sophomore year, i took two advanced college-level cs algorithms/data structures courses at bunker hill community college, covering topics like binary trees, linked lists, and hashing. after completing them, i wanted to apply those concepts to real-world problems, which inspired me to pursue my machine learning independent study.
